Based on positive personal experience and positive feedback from the community about our open submission and publication model (ACM DL / arXiv). where papers, artifacts and experimental results are submitted directly via ArXiv and then openly discussed and pre-validated by the community, we decided to use it exclusively for ADAPT 2016 (see ADAPT motivation page).

We invite two types of papers:

  • T1: Research Papers (between 2 and 8 pages long) presenting novel techniques including wild & crazy ideas.
  • T2: Software/Hardware Demonstration (must be your own) presenting publicly available and adaptive applications, tools and hardware. Description should be up to 6 pages long summarizing demonstration. The relevance to the workshop should be specified in the abstract submitted via EasyChair.

Submissions should be in PDF, preferably formatted with double column/single-spacing, using 10pt fonts and printable on US letter or A4 sized paper.

Authors submit a paper via ADAPT 2016 EasyChair website at any time before the submission deadline (please provide above prefix T1 or T2 in the title). We will immediately give authors instructions about how to upload their paper to arXiv (allow 2..3 days before your article becomes available online). Alternatively, authors can submit their paper directly to ArXiv and then submit an abstract to EasyChair with the link to their paper. As soon as this paper is publicly available, we open a discussion thread on the Reddit social platform to allow the community to evaluate and comment on it (the authors can respond to all the comments at any time to promote, explain and defend their technique). Note that we encourage earlier submissions before the deadline to allow more time for open discussions. We also allow submission of your own papers already available in any open archive if they are related to ADAPT topics.

After three weeks of public discussions, we will close the discussion thread and let the Program Committee members reach their acceptance decisions based on their own evaluation and from the comments of others (including the authors) found on the discussion thread. Papers accepted for presentation at the workshop may later include a link to the public reddit discussion.

Importantly, ADAPT workshop has a special focus on practical, scalable and reproducible techniques. Hence, we strongly encourage authors to share their packed artifacts (code, data, models, experimental results) and provide a paragraph in their submitted papers describing how to validate their technique and experimental results together with a link to their artifacts. Paper with highest ranked artifacts shared in Collective Knowledge format will receive a cash prize of 200 euros from our sponsor (see example of a CK-powered paper).
